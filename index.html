About Kenya Veterans for Peace

Founded by veterans who believe their duty to protect Kenya extends beyond the uniform.

Our Story

Who We Are

Kenya Veterans for Peace (KVP) was founded by former military and security officers who saw firsthand how conflict destroys communities — and believed they had a responsibility to use their experience for peace instead.

We are affiliated with Veterans For Peace International and work alongside government agencies, civil society organizations, and community leaders across all 47 counties of Kenya.

Our motto — "Peace Begins With You" — reflects our belief that lasting peace starts with individual commitment and spreads outward into families, communities, and nations.

Our Values

What We Stand For

NonviolenceWe renounce violence as a means of resolving conflict and advocate for peaceful alternatives in all situations.

AccountabilityWe hold ourselves and institutions to the highest standards of honesty, transparency, and responsibility.

InclusivityPeace belongs to all Kenyans regardless of tribe, faith, gender, or background — no one is excluded.

ServiceOur duty to protect Kenya did not end with our discharge papers; it simply changed its form.

Our Programs

Four pillars of peacebuilding, delivered by veterans who understand the human cost of conflict.

Peace Education

KVP veterans visit schools, youth centers, and community halls to deliver structured peace education curricula. Facilitators draw on lived experience to make lessons real and transformative.

200+ schools  ·  30,000+ students reached

Community Mediation

Trained veteran mediators work in conflict hotspots, facilitating dialogue between communities, families, and local factions to resolve disputes before they escalate to violence.

15 counties  ·  500+ cases resolved

PTSD Support

Many veterans carry invisible wounds. Our peer-led mental health program provides safe spaces, counselling referrals, and group therapy to help veterans process trauma and heal.

1,200+ supported  ·  8 active support groups

Youth Mentorship

Veterans mentor at-risk youth, offering guidance, discipline, and positive role models. Many participants go on to become community leaders and peace ambassadors themselves.

3,000+ mentees  ·  24 counties
